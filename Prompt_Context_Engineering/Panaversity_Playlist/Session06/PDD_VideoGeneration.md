## Prompt Driven Development (PDD) VS Vibe Coding

### Prompt-Driven Development (PDD)

* **You tell the AI exactly what you want. Very clear. Very specific.**
* Like giving your friend a recipe with step-by-step instructions.

* ‚ÄúMake a login page in Next.js with email + password fields, Tailwind styling, and form validation.‚Äù

### Vibe Coding

* **You give the AI a rough idea, and it figures out the rest based on ‚Äúvibes.‚Äù**
* Like saying to a friend: ‚ÄúMake it look cool and modern.‚Äù

*Example:*
* ‚ÄúMake a nice login page that feels clean.‚Äù
* The AI uses creativity ‚Üí results vary.

**In short:**
üëâ *Looser instructions, more freedom.*
üëâ *Good for brainstorming or quick drafts.*

**Side-by-Side**
| Prompt-Driven Development (PDD) | Vibe Coding |
|---------------------------------|-------------|
| Clear, detailed instructions     | Loose, creative instructions |
| Predictable and consistent output | Surprising or varied output |
| Best for production-grade work   | Best for exploration and brainstorming |
| You control every step           | AI fills in the blanks |

### Core Principles
1) Cinematic Specificity Over Generic Descriptions
2) Narrative Structure
3) Professional Film Language

### Anatomy of Effective Veo 3 Prompts
A well-structured prompt typically includes:

1) **Camera Movement/Angle** (establishing the shot)
2) **Subject Description** (who/what is in frame)
3) **Action/Movement** (what's happening)
4) **Environment/Setting** (where it takes place)
5) **Lighting/Atmosphere** (mood and visual tone)
6) **Audio Elements** (dialogue, sound effects, ambient)
7) **Technical Style** (film format, color grading)
8) **Duration Pacing** (how action unfolds over time)
